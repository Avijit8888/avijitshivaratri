Chapter I

Singularity

Before time had a name, before space unfolded its vast canvas, before stars burned or atoms formed, there existed a singularity — infinite density compressed within infinite silence. Science describes it as the beginning of the universe. Spiritual wisdom calls it Adi Parashakti — the primordial energy. All matter, all light, all consciousness rested within a boundless point of potential.

Chapter II

The First Expansion

The singularity expanded. Space stretched. Time began to flow. Energy transformed into particles, particles into atoms, atoms into stars and galaxies. Creation was not chaos — it was movement from stillness into expression. The universe unfolded with hidden order.

Chapter III

Shakti

Energy vibrated through the cosmos. Gravity bound stars. Light pierced the void. Nebulae swirled like breath in motion. This dynamic force — the pulse that moves galaxies and ignites suns — is Shakti.

Chapter IV

Shiva

Beyond motion lies awareness. The silent observer. The stillness beneath vibration. This eternal witness is Shiva — pure consciousness itself.

Chapter V

The Sacred Union

For creation to exist, unity appeared divided. Consciousness and energy seemed separate, yet remained eternally one. Like space and time. Like love and longing. What separates in form remains united in essence.

Chapter VI

Love

From separation arose longing. From longing arose devotion. Love became the gravity of existence — the invisible force bending destiny.

Chapter VII

Eternal

What began as singularity lives within every atom, within every heartbeat, within every act of love. What is eternal does not end. It transforms. It returns. It remembers.
